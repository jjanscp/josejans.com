I've rebuilt this site as a more academic home for the work I'm doing as a PhD student in Political Science at the University of Illinois Chicago and as a Fulbright–MinCiencias Scholar.

The previous version of **josejans.com** (online since 2018) was primarily a Spanish-language professional site from my years as a public-interest lawyer in Colombia — with the Special Jurisdiction for Peace (JEP), the José Alvear Restrepo Lawyers' Collective (CAJAR), and the International Federation for Human Rights (FIDH). None of that work has been deleted: it has been re-framed and moved to the [Research Archive](/en/archive/), where it sits as the empirical foundation of my current research agenda.

What's new here:

- A dedicated **[Publications](/en/publications/)** section organized by scholarly category — peer-reviewed articles, book chapters, policy and human rights reports, amicus curiae briefs, and works in progress.
- A **[Research](/en/research/)** page that connects my doctoral agenda (transitional justice, rights mobilization, the politics of accountability) to a decade of practice.
- This **Blog**, which I'll use for short-form notes on research in progress, teaching, and occasional commentary on Colombian politics and the courts.

Thanks for stopping by. You can reach me at [jcarr74@uic.edu](mailto:jcarr74@uic.edu) or through the [contact page](/en/contact/).
